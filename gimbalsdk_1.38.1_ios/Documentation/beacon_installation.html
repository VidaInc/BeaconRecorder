Gimbal Beacon Installation Best Practices

Selecting the Right Beacon

Foreground vs. Background

If you expect your App to see beacons while it is in the background (e.g. someone's pocket), use series S20.

Durability

Series S20 beacons run on 4 AA batteries and are suitable for outdoor use as well as indoor.

Battery Life

S20s have a typical battery life of 1 year for both foreground and background app use cases.

Cost

Please contact your Gimbal Business Development to discuss pricing.

Beacon Placement

Physical Security

It is preferable to install beacons out of normal human reach (~ 10 - 12ft high). For added security with the S20s, use the 'set screw' to help prevent tampering.

Signal Propagation

Place the beacon where it will have good visibility to the end user’s phone. A clear, consistent, unobstructed signal is the best way to achieve consistent app behavior.

Battery Replacement

For more permanent installations, consider how easy it will be to replace batteries later. Can it be easily reached from a ladder? Can the S20s be opened while mounted? If the ‘set screw’ has been used, is there room for a screwdriver in order to open it?

Presence vs. Running

Consider what use cases your deployment needs to support. If your app is trying to determine whether someone is present or not, focus on placing beacons to prevent leakage outside of that area. If your app needs to know how close they are, assess the specific amount of area you’re trying to cover and increase the density/number of beacons you’re deploying until your use cases work consistently.

Beacon Installation

Physical Tools

A long pole can be useful for testing (tape a beacon to the end of the pole to simulate placement during testing). We also recommend having some Gaff tape handy (no residue, made of cloth and doesn’t impede the beacon’s antenna). In many cases, a ladder is also required for installations depending on where you’re placing beacons. Finally, if you’re using the ‘set screw’ on the S20s, make sure you have a Torx T8 screwdriver handy.

Software Tools

An iPhone 5 or above is recommended for testing and should be running the Gimbal Beacon Manager (GBM) application (available for free from the app store). GBM can be used to configure beacons as necessary as well as to sight beacons during installation and testing.

Mounting

In most cases, the double-sided tape included with each beacon is sufficient but consider using the ¼”-20 mount or drywall screws supported by the S20 beacon, as applicable.
